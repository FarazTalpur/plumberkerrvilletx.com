Frequently Asked Questions

A sump pump is a device used to remove water that has accumulated in a water-collecting sump basin, typically found in the basements of homes. It is essential for preventing flooding in areas prone to heavy rain, high water tables, or poor drainage.

The size of the sump pump depends on the area of your basement, the depth of your basement, and the volume of water that needs to be removed. A professional inspection can determine your specific needs, but generally, a 1/3 to 1/2 horsepower pump suffices for most homes.

While it is possible to install a sump pump yourself if you have basic plumbing skills, it is recommended to hire a professional to ensure proper installation, which is crucial for effective operation and compliance with local building codes.

Regular maintenance includes checking the pump's operation, cleaning the discharge line and sump pit, testing the float switch, and inspecting the power source twice a year. Battery backups should also be checked and replaced as needed.

The lifespan of a sump pump is generally 7-10 years, depending on usage, maintenance, and the quality of the pump. Regular maintenance can help extend the life of the pump.
